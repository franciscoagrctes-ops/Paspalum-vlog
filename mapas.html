Main conclusions

of the study
Distribution

Ensemble models place the potential distribution of the species mainly in southern Brazil, eastern Paraguay, northeastern Argentina, and northern Uruguay — a tighter core area than the one suggested by raw herbarium records.

— (Luna et al., 2026)

Taxonomy

Records from tropical latitudes that fall outside this core likely reflect cultivated populations or misidentifications within the taxonomically difficult Plicatula group, rather than a true extension of the natural range.

— (Luna et al., 2026)

Precipitation

Precipitation was the strongest driver of the species' ecological niche: suitability falls sharply once the wettest month exceeds roughly 200 mm, which also explains why tropical, high-rainfall areas are poorly suited to the species.

— (Luna et al., 2026)

Temperature

Mean diurnal temperature range was another key predictor, pointing to a preference for temperate climates with wide daily thermal swings — consistent with the species' cold tolerance and year-round forage production.

— (Luna et al., 2026)

Soil and Elevation

Soil and elevation played only a secondary role. The species tends toward acidic-to-neutral, moisture-retentive soils, but tolerates a fairly wide range of elevations and soil fertility levels.

— (Luna et al., 2026)

Conservation

The study highlights the need to increase collection efforts in the Brazilian states of Paraná and Rio Grande do Sul.

— (Luna et al., 2026)
