Environmental Preferences

Environmental variable response curves from the species distribution model. Hover over each panel to see its interpretation.

Species in view Paspalum guenoarum
Precipitation of Wettest Month Suitability is high when the wettest month receives roughly 100–200 mm, dropping sharply above 250–300 mm and staying low above 350–400 mm. The species appears to favor a humid — but not extremely rainy — wettest month; excess rainfall during that month may limit its distribution.
Mean Diurnal Range Suitability rises notably once the daily temperature range exceeds about 7 °C, peaking between 9 and 12 °C. The species is associated with environments showing a moderate-to-high difference between daily maximum and minimum temperatures.
Annual Precipitation Suitability is low below 800–1000 mm/year, rises quickly to a peak around 1400–1800 mm/year, then gradually declines above 2000–2500 mm/year. The species prefers humid environments with intermediate-to-high annual rainfall, avoiding both dry and extremely wet regions.
Precipitation of Warmest Quarter Suitability increases up to roughly 400–600 mm during the warmest quarter, then slowly declines at higher values. Growth seems to be favored when the warm season has abundant water available, though excess rainfall reduces suitability.
Soil pH The highest suitability occurs between pH 5.2 and 5.8, decreasing progressively above pH 6. The species shows a preference for slightly acidic soils and is less common in near-neutral soils.
Cation Exchange Capacity (CEC) Peak suitability occurs at roughly 10–15 cmol(+)/kg, decreasing at values above 20–25 cmol(+)/kg. The species appears to favor soils of moderate fertility, avoiding soils with very high exchange capacities.
Available Water Storage Capacity Suitability peaks around 40–44 mm and gradually decreases once capacity exceeds roughly 45–46 mm. The species prefers soils with intermediate water-holding capacity, avoiding both very dry soils and those with excessive retention.
Elevation Suitability increases from sea level up to about 800–1200 m, then progressively declines above 1500–2000 m, becoming low beyond 3000 m. The species shows greater affinity for lowlands and mid-elevation areas, being poorly suited to high-mountain environments.
